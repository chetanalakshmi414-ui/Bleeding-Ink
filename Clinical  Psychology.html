WHAT EXACTLY IS A PHOBIA?

A phobia is an intense and irrational fear caused by an object, which affects the person's behaviour and makes that specific individual anxious about a specific thing. Many people consider phobia as some phenomenon of exaggeration but in reality it can cause many possible mood swings and affect a person's mental health if that person gets no help or attention.A phobia can be born due to a past horrible experience as well. Example: Fear of water:Hydrophobia

A BRIEF ABOUT OBSESSIVE-COMPULSIVE DISORDER

Obsessive–Compulsive Disorder (OCD) is a mental health condition where a person gets repeated, unwanted thoughts or fears (called obsessions) and feels pushed to do certain actions or rituals (called compulsions) to reduce the anxiety or discomfort those thoughts create. These thoughts and actions feel hard to control, take up time, and can get in the way of daily life — even though the person usually knows the behaviour doesn’t logically make sense. In cases, the person suffering from this disorder can want everything to be in a precise position,tidy,neat and everything clean.

IMPACT:

Though ocd doesn't fully affect the mental health of a person- it usually has few minor negative consequences-

1)LACK OF FOCUS/CONCENTRATION

2)CONSTANT RESTLESSNESS

3)ANXIETY
