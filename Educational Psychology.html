EDUCATION IS THE MOST POWERFUL WEAPON THAT CAN BE USED TO CHANGE THE WORLD. THERE IS ALWAYS AN ACCURATE PSYCHOLOGY BEHIND AN EFFICIENT AND SERENE EDUCATION. Education isn't always about books, papers and stationary, it also lies in memories you make throughout the course. Things can be learnt in different ways; through practicals, activities, group discussions and seminars.

EFFECTIVE STUDY METHODS FOR STUDENTS~

1)ACTIVE RECALL: An effective study method in which a student learns, memorizes everything and a few moments later, they collectively recall all the information they have learnt and it buids the pathway to your long-term memory.
2)THE BLURTING METHOD: It is another study method, which illustrates that- when a person finishes reading, he immediately puts down all the information he remembers on a paper and underlines the points he has missed in writing.
3)THE FENYMAN TECHNIQUE: Another common technique practised by most of the students for proper conceptual understanding and clarity. In this case, a person learns a concept and explains it to the other person or a non-living entity. THE MORE YOU EXPLAIN THINGS TO SOMEONE IS THE MORE UNDERSTANDING YOU ACQUIRE FROM THE TOPIC.
4)THE POMODORO TECHNIQUE:This kind of practise is very much efficient for procrastinators. This specific thing, involves 25 minutes of focused studying and 5 minutes of break. At certain times, this technique may not work for the ones who are always distracted by some or the other thing.
5)THE SPACED REPETITION: A person studies a thing, closes the book and after a couple of hours he would open the book again and revise. This space can include hours or weeks as well but it depends on your short and long term memory.

THEORY OF MUGGING UP CONCEPTS (ROTE REPETITION)

ROTE REPETITION CANNOT BE CONSIDERED AS A WAY OF LEARNING BUT THE EXACT OPPOSITE OF IT.YOU JUST MUG UP THINGS WITHOUT ACTUALLY UNDERSTANDING THEM, IT CAN ACTUALLY LEAD YOU TO DISASTROUS CONSEQUENCES- like forgetting the concept in a short span of time; mixing up one concept with another which could make no sense and sound confusing. Rote repetion can sound ordinary or really common to few people out there- you may mug up the things but it would only be stored in your short-term memory (which again correlates with cognitive development); and can have all the possible adverse consequences in future.

A BRIEF ABOUT CONSTRUCTIVISM AND BEHAVIOURISM

CONSTRUCTIVISM: It is a learning theory based on the educational and cognitive psychology- constructivism clearly illustrates the softer practise pursued either by the learner or teacher

CONSTRUCTIVISM INCLUDES THE FOLLOWING HABITS: (SELF-STUDY)
1)ROMANTICIZING STUDY SESSIONS: Taking your own time to understand concepts, having your favorite snack while studying and listening to soulful music which soothenes your heart.
2)PREPARING COLOURFUL NOTES AND KEYPOINTS WHILE STUDYING: Using highlighters, colourful pens, sketches and other convenient things come under constructivism in self-study
3)ACTIVE RECALL (an effective study method)
4)SETTING A NON-HECTIC STUDY SCHEDULE (pampering yourself in simple terms)
Positive Negative
1) DEVELOPMENT OF HIGHER-ORDER THINKING SKILLS (you do it yourself and learn from experiences) 1) MAY ONLY BE STORED IN YOUR SHORT-TERM MEMORY
2)NO ANXIETY ISSUES 2)THE PERSON MAY BE EXPOSED TO DISTRACTIONS IF THE MINDSET IS POOR.

A lot of consequences of constructivism aren't shown in this page because it totally depends on the person's mindset and the type of study method he/she is implying.

BEHAVIOURISM

BEHAVIORISM HOLDS A LOT OF POWER AND IT CAN BE CONSIDERED AS SOMETHING OPPOSITE TO CONSTRUCTIVISM. WHEN WE EMPHASIZE ABOUT BEHAVIOURISM IT CAN BE CATEGORIZED SUBTLE WAYS BUT AS OF NOW, WE WOULD ONLY CONSIDER ONE WAY, WHICH IS THE WAY OF LEARNING.

BEHAVIOURISM INCLUDES THE FOLLOWING HABITS~

1) LEARNING HAPPENS FROM OUTSIDE (it is caused by external stimuli)

2)CONDITIONING: CLASSICAL CONDITIONING AND OPERANT CONDITIONING

DIFFERNCE BETWEEN CLASSICAL CONDITIONING AND OPERANT CONDITIONING:

CLASSICAL CONDITIONING OPERANT CONDITIONING
THE BEHAVIOUR IS INVOLUNTARY THE BEHAVIOUR IS VOLUNTARY
LEARNER IS PASSIVE LEARNER IS ACTIVE
LEARNING THROUGH ASSOCIATION OF TWO STIMULI LEARNING HAPPENS BY CONSEQUENCES LIKE REWARD OR PUNISHMENT
FOR EXAMPLE: SALIVATION OF DOG; AS SOON AS DOG HEARS THE BELL IT IMMEDIATELY RUSHES TO THE PERSON GIVING FOOD; THIS WAS BECAUSE THE DOG HAS PREVIOUSLY WITNESSED THIS EXPERIENCE AND NOW TWO STIMULI GET ASSOCIATED AND THE ACTION IS OBSERVED FOR EXAMPLE: A student immediately begins to study when he/she is either rewarded or punished; the brain starts to imagine the worst case ever possible because of the previous experience he/she has witnessed and the behaviour becomes voluntary resulting in the operant conditioning
KEYPOINTS TO BE NOTED:
1)THE BEHAVIOUR MAY DIFFER FROM PERSON TO PERSON DEPENDING UPON THE TRAITS HE/SHE BEARS
2)DEPENDS ON THE ENVIRONMENT HE/SHE HAS BEEN RAISED IN AND THE CIRCUMSTANCES THEY'VE WITNESSED IN THAT SPECIFIED AREA
3)DEPENDS ON THE AGE OF THE PERSON- IT COULD BE AN ADOLESCENT KID, A TOODLER OR EVEN AN ADULT
4)DEPENDS ON THE TEACHER AND THE LISTENER AS WELL
5)THE TEACHER'S EFFORTS AND THE LISTENER'S COOPERATION COULD CONTRIBUTE TO THE MASSIVE VIRTUE OF EDUCATION
