STRUCTURE OF A NEURON

THE NERVOUS TISSUE CONSTITUTES THE NERVOUS SYSTEM, IT IS MADE UP OF SPECIALISED ELONGATED CELLS CALLED NEURONS. THERE ARE MILLIONS OF NEURONS PRESENT IN THE BRAIN WHICH TRANSMIT IMPULSES (NERVE SIGNALS) FROM THE BRAIN TO VARIOUS PARTS OF THE BODY.IT IS BECAUSE OF THE NEURONS; WE ARE ABLE TO RESPOND TO CIRCUMSTANCES OR SIGNALS. FOR EXAMPLE: WHEN WE TOUCH A HOT GLASS OF WATER, WE INSTANTLY TAKE OFF OUR HANDS FROM IT DUE TO THE DEGREE OF HOTNESS- HERE, THE NERVE IMPULSES ARE TRANSMITTED FROM THE FINGERS TO THE BRAIN -SPECIFICALLY TERMED AS SENSORY NEURONS.IT SENDS A MESSAGE TO THE BRAIN AND THUS YOU TEND TO REMOVE YOUR HAND IMMEDIATELY, SINCE THE SKIN HAS WITNESSED A BURNING SENSATION.

PARTS OF A NEURON
1)CYTON/CELL BODY: Cyton consists of a nucleus present in the centre, the cell body also consists of nissl granules
2) DENDRONS AND DENDRITES: Dendrons are shorter, tapering and branched processes that protrude from the cell body.They receive nerve impulses from other neurons. On the other hand, dendrites are fine and terminal branches that emerge from the dendrons, they transmit nerve impulses to the dendrons and the cell body as well.
3)THE MYELIN SHEATH: Oftentimes, it acts as an insulator and prevents the mixing of nerve impulses.
4)AXON: An axon is a long elongated structure which is composed of a myelin sheath; axon serves as a pathway for the transmission of impulses either from brain to various parts of the body or from the different parts of the body to the brain.
5)THE AXON ENDINGS: They contain synaptic vessels which store the neurotransmitters, when an electric signal reaches this- the synaptic vessel activates the neurotransmitter.

FUNCTIONING OF NEURON WITH RESPECT TO COGNITION:

1)RECEPTION OF INFORMATION~ WE OBTAIN INFORMATION THROUGH SENSES AND FEW BY OUR DEEPEST INSTINCTS AS WELL. THESE MESSAGES ARE OBTAINED BY THE NERVE ENDINGS AND ARE TRANSMITTED TO THE CELL BODY; WHERE IT PROCESSES THE INFORMATION AND THE AXON ACTS LIKE A PATHWAY.

2)DECODING~ THE RECEIVER INTERPRETS THE MESSAGE TO CONVERT IT AS A THOUGHT, AN IDEA OR A LESSON.

3)UNDERSTANDING~THE USER UNDERSTANDS THE INFORMATION AND STORES IT EITHER IN YOUR SHORT/LONG TERM MEMORY DEPENDING UPON THE WAY YOU INPUT THE INFORMATION
